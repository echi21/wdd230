Freedom Square

Central square of the city. It was remodeled in 1936. It has been declared a national historical monument.

Surrounding Squares

The surrounding squares are very well forested and have wide spaces with shade produced by their leafy trees. It has pergolas, sculptures, fountains, planters, many plants and flowers.

Juan Manuel Fangio Museum

After incessant work, the Technological-Cultural Center and Automobile Museum Juan Manuel Fangio was presented to the world on November 22, 1986. It is a museum dedicated to the aforementioned driver, located in the center of the city, who was a five-time Formula 1 champion.

The Triumph Hill

The Triumph Hill is one of the most beautiful public places in the city of Balcarce. One where you can enjoy with family or friends. It offers accessible roads to travel by car, restroom infrastructure, a sports center with an athletics track, a velodrome and a basketball court, a grocery store, grills, children's games, etc.

La Brava Lake

Located within Mount La Brava, the lake area is privately owned, although open to the public for fishing and camping. There are several campsites around the lake, like Ruca Lauquen on the east coast and the Fishing Club Balcarce on the north, both of them with recreational facilities and boat piers. The lake is described as a rift lake or tectonic lake, probably a natural reservoir formed by dunes. La Brava is the habitat of a number of bird species, like swans, ducks, coots, herons, and gulls. Capybaras and otters dwell on its shores. The lake also supports a fish community dominated by the silverside and the dientudo.

Balcarce city

San José de Balcarce (shortened to Balcarce) is a city in Buenos Aires Province (Argentina) about 51 km (32 mi) west of Mar del Plata. It is the head town of the District of Balcarce. The city is famous as the birthplace of Formula One legend Juan Manuel Fangio and today houses the Juan Manuel Fangio Museum, and the Juan Manuel Fangio Racetrack, a motorsports circuit. The town hall, cemetery portal and slaughterhouse were all designed by the architect, Francisco Salamone, and contain elements of Art Deco style. Built in the late 1930s, these buildings were some of the first examples of modern architecture in rural Argentina.
